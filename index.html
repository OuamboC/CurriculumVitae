ABOUT

After completing my Baccalaureate in Cameroon, I moved to the United Kingdom with the unwavering support of my parents to continue my studies. I spent my first year at Into Manchester, learning English, starting in September 2019. It was a challenging but rewarding year as I adjusted to a new culture and environment.

When the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted our studies, it was a frustrating time, but we quickly adapted to online classes, which taught me the value of resilience and flexibility. In 2021, I completed my Foundation Year in Science and Engineering with Triple A* grades, and I was thrilled to receive an offer to study Chemical Engineering at the University of Manchester.

However, as I embarked on my journey into Chemical Engineering, my path started to shift. I was drawn to technology and digital innovation, especially after a series of experiences: creating a Shopify website while exploring dropshipping, learning MATLAB in university, and diving into the world of blockchain (Web3). These experiences sparked a desire to pursue a career in technology and software development.

Though my family and tutors didn't recommend it, I made the difficult decision to change my course and university. It was a leap into the unknown, but it felt right. This journey is what led me to study Software Engineering, and I haven't looked back since.

Another key factor in my decision was my role in helping my family business transition online. I had the opportunity to propose and work on digitizing our business, which further fueled my passion for technology and reinforced my decision to pursue a career in software engineering.
